Listen and Repeat

Introducción

En esta sección escucharás conversaciones de diferentes personas y situaciones, para que te familiarices con el idioma.
No te preocupes si no entiendes cada una de las palabras, ¡trata de comprender qué es lo que quieren decir!
Si lo deseas, puedes apoyarte con un diccionario para buscar las palabras clave en cada conversación.
¡Adelante, hazlo, tú puedes! Es uno de los ejercicios más recomendados para aprender a expresarte oralmente en el inglés.

Listening


Instrucciones: Escucha detenidamente el diálogo que se presenta a continuación. Debes escucharlo las veces que sea necesario, hasta que te sientas confiado en que comprendiste lo que las personas están diciendo.

Diálogo 1

An emergency call / Una llamada de emergencia

An emergency call / Una llamada de emergencia

Police: Hello! Police Department. Can I help you?
Woman: Yes, I have an emergency..
 Police:What is the problem?
 Woman:My cat is stuck in the tree and will not come down.
 Police:: I need some information from you. What is your name?
 Woman: My name is Norma Jones.
 Police:What is your address?
 Woman: My address is 322 N. Main Street.
 Police: What is your telephone number?
Woman: My telephone number is 765-4321.
Police: We’re on our way.
Woman:Thank you officer.

Diálogo 2

A speeding ticket / Multa de transito

A speeding ticket / Multa de transito

Police: Do you know how fast you were driving?
Woman: Was I driving too fast?
 Police: Very fast. I’m going to have to give you a ticket. What’s your name?
 Woman: Arthur Miller.
 Police: What’s your address?
 Woman: 158 Glory Lane.
 Police: Is that a house or an apartment?
 Woman: It’s a house.
 Police: What’s your phone number?
 Woman: 984-9347.
 Police: Here’s your ticket. Next time don’t drive so fast.
 Woman: All right, officer.

Diálogo 3

Applying for a job / Solicitando un trabajo

Applying for a job / Solicitando un trabajo

Old woman: I would like to apply for a job in the newspaper.
Clerk: Here is the application.
 Old woman: What? Talk louder. I can’t hear you.
 Clerk: Here’s the application. Fill in the information about yourself.
 Old woman: Can you help me fill in the information? I can’t see very well.
 Clerk: Okay.
 Old woman: What?
 Clerk: I said “okay”. What’s your name?
Old woman: My name? Oh yes. My name is Ruth Carson.
 Clerk: What’s your address?
Old woman: What color is my dress? My dress is red. Why do they want to know what color my dress is?
 Clerk: Not your “dress”. Your address, what is your address?
Old woman: My address is 634 Beecher Street.
 Clerk: What is your phone number?
Old woman: I don’t have a telephone
 Clerk: You don’t have a telephone? How can we contact you?
Old woman: I changed my mind. I don’t want to work here anymore.
 Clerk: Good bye. Have a nice day.
Old woman: What? Did you tell me to stay? I’m sorry. I have to go home now. Good bye.
