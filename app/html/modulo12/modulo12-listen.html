PEOPLE, PLACES, AND THINGS

Listen and Repeat

En esta sección escucharás conversaciones de diferentes personas y situaciones, para que te familiarices con el idioma.
No te preocupes si no entiendes cada una de las palabras, ¡trata de comprender qué es lo que quieren decir!
Si lo deseas, puedes apoyarte con un diccionario para buscar las palabras clave en cada conversación.
¡Adelante, hazlo, tú puedes! Es uno de los ejercicios más recomendados para aprender a expresarte oralmente en el inglés.

Diálogo 1

A tiger / Un tigre


Meeting people at school / Conociendo gente en la escuela

Rosita: Mother, what is that?
Mother: Thats a tiger.
 Rosita: It's beautiful. I like the colors: it is orange with black stripes. It looks very soft. Can I touch it?
 Mother:No, Rosita. A tiger is a dangerous animal. It has sharp claws and sharp teeth.
 Rosita: Can it eat me?

 Rosita: Can it run fast?.

Diálogo 2

A sailboat / Un velero


A sailboat / Un velero

Jeff: Look at that sailboat.
Michael: Wow! It's really fast.
 Jeff: What color are the sails?
 Michael: They are blue, red and white.
 Jeff: It is moving very fast. It is a windy day.
 Michael: Yes, it is a beautiful day. The sky is blue an there are no clouds.
 Jeff: I want to go on the sailboat.
 Michael: Not me! I get seasick!

Diálogo 3

Excuse me, may I? / ¿Disculpe, podria?

Excuse me, may I? / ¿Disculpe, podria?

Patty: Is that Dr. Roman?
 Marco: I'm not sure. What does Dr. Roman look like?
 Patty: She has a long white jacket. She is not tall ad not short. She has long brown hair.
 Marco: Is she wearing glasses?
 Patty: Yes, she is.
 Marco: That must be her. But this doctor has short hair. Dr. Roman has long hair. Let's ask her.
 Patty: Excuse me, are you Dr. Roman?
 Dr. Roman: Yes, I am Dr. Roman.
 Marco: You look different.
 Dr. Roman: Yes, I know. I used to have long hair, but now my hair is short.
 Marco: Dr. Roman, I am Marco Cortez.
 Dr. Roman: You look different too.
 Marco: Yes, I know. I used to have a mustache and a beard. Now I don't.
 Dr. Roman Nice to see you again, Marco.
 Marco: Nice to see you, too.
