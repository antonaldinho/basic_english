Listening

Listen and Repeat

En esta sección escucharás conversaciones de diferentes personas y situaciones, para que te familiarices con el idioma.
No te preocupes si no entiendes cada una de las palabras, ¡trata de comprender qué es lo que quieren decir!
Si lo deseas, puedes apoyarte con un diccionario para buscar las palabras clave en cada conversación.
¡Adelante, hazlo, tú puedes! Es uno de los ejercicios más recomendados para aprender a expresarte oralmente en el inglés.

Listening

Dialogue A

Telephone Company / Compañia Telefónica

Telephone Company / Compañia Telefónica

Jenny: Hello.
Sandra: Hello Is this Jenny Andreson?
 Jenny: Hi! My name is Sandra Hopkins. I’m calling you today to see if you would like to change your long distance telephone service. We have a great offer for you.
 Jenny: No thanks, I’m very happy with my present long distance service.
 Sandra: Okay. Thanks, anyway. Have a nice day.
 Jenny: Bye.

Dialogue B

Hanging with Friends / Saliendo con Amigos

Hanging with Friends / Saliendo con Amigos

Alex: Hello
John: Hello, Alex? This is John. How are you?
Alex: Oh, hi, John. I’m fine thanks. How are you?
 John: Fine. Maria and I want to go to the movies tomorrow night at the Rose Cinema. They’re showing, “The Fiasco” and we wanted to know if you and Carla wanted to join us.
Alex: Well, it sounds like a great idea but I have to talk to Carla first. What time is the movie showing?
 John: The show times are 5:15, 7:30 or 9:15.
Alex: Maybe we could go to the 5:15 show and then we could go out for dinner.
John: : That sounds great! I’ll tell Maria and you talk to Carla. Could you call me back by 8:00? I have to go and pick up Luis from a friend’s house.
Alex: Sure. I’ll talk to her right now and call you right back.
John: Great. Talk to you soon.
Alex: Bye.

Dialogue C

Making Plans / Haciendo Planes

Making Plans / Haciendo Planes

Daniel: Hello?
Rita: Hello. Daniel. It’s Rita. How are you?
 Daniel: Hello. Daniel. It’s Rita. How are you?
 Daniel: Just fine, thanks. What’s up?
 Rita: Marco, Gina and I were wondering if you wanted to go bowling with us at about 8:00. Are you busy?
 Daniel: Well, actually I am. I have a math exam on Tuesday and I really need to study. I have to study and if I go bowling, I won’t get too much accomplished. Are you going anywhere after you are finished bowling?
 Rita: We were thinking about going to “Los Amigos” for a couple of drinks.
 Daniel: Listen. I´ll study for the exam until about 10:00. I’ll meet you after bowling at “Los Amigos” at about 10:30.
 Rita: Okay, I’m sorry you can’t make it for bowling, but I understand. Well, see you later.
Daniel: Okay. See you later.
 Rita: Bye.
